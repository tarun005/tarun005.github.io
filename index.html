Tarun Kalluri

I am a first-year PhD student in the CSE department at UCSD. My research interests span the areas of computer vision and machine learning. I am particularly interested in learning representations of images and videos from limited labeled data that are useful and transferable across a wide range of domains. Our group at UCSD works on a wider variety of problems in computer vision involving graphics, 3D reconstruction, material estimation and robotics.

Prior to this, I was a research assistant at Centre for Visual Information and Technology (CVIT), IIIT Hyderabad advised by Prof. CV Jawahar . I worked on various problems related to semantic understanding from large scale text and image data using techniques in deep learning.

Even before that, I spent a year working as a data scientist at Oracle India, Bangalore. I completed my undergraduation from IIT Guwahati in 2016, with a major in ECE and a minor in CSE. I did my bachelor's thesis on energy harvesting in wireless networks , a field that has kept me busy for long before I started pursuing deep learning.

    This manuscript presents some preliminary ideas regarding the reconstruction of Proto-Modern South Arabian, which is of interest for the reconstruction of Proto-Semitic as a whole. A more thorough analysis will have to wait for a comprehensive documentation of Soqotri, Hobyot, and of dialectal variation within the better-documented languages Mehri and Jibbali.
